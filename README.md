🚖 Uber Ride Booking Analysis
📊 Project Overview

This project provides a comprehensive analysis of Uber ride booking data, focusing on booking performance, cancellation behavior, payment trends, and ride demand patterns over time.

The goal of this analysis is to uncover operational inefficiencies, understand customer and driver behavior, and generate insights that can improve ride completion rates and overall service performance.

🎯 Business Objectives
Evaluate overall booking performance
Analyze ride completion and cancellation trends
Identify key reasons for ride cancellations
Understand ride demand patterns over time
Assess customer payment preferences
Provide actionable insights for operational improvement
📈 Key Performance Metrics
Metric	Value
Total Bookings	150,000
Completed Rides	93,000
Cancelled Rides	38,000
📊 Ride Volume Trend
March recorded the highest ride volume: 4,568,188

🔎 Insight:
Ride demand peaked significantly in March, indicating a possible seasonal surge, promotional campaign impact, or increased user activity during that period.

❌ Cancellation Analysis
Customer Ride Cancellation
The primary reason for customer cancellations is:
Wrong Address

🔎 Insight:
This suggests usability or input issues in the booking process, highlighting the need for improved location validation or UI enhancements.

Driver Ride Cancellation
Driver cancellations are largely driven by:
Customer-related issues

🔎 Insight:
This may include factors such as:

Incorrect pickup details
Delays from customers
Communication challenges

This indicates a need for better coordination and real-time communication between drivers and customers.

💳 Payment Method Analysis
UPI is the most used payment method with over 23M transactions/value

🔎 Insight:
The strong dominance of UPI reflects:

High customer trust in digital payments
Convenience and speed of transactions
Growing adoption of cashless ecosystems
📊 Key Business Insights
A significant gap exists between total bookings and completed rides, indicating operational inefficiencies.
Cancellation rates are notably high, requiring targeted intervention.
Customer input errors (wrong address) are a major friction point.
Driver cancellations linked to customer issues suggest coordination gaps.
Digital payments (UPI) dominate, highlighting an opportunity to further incentivize cashless transactions.
Ride demand is highly time-sensitive, with clear monthly peaks.

📌 Recommendations
Implement address validation systems to reduce incorrect bookings
Improve in-app communication between drivers and riders
Introduce penalty or incentive systems to reduce cancellations
Leverage peak demand periods (March) for targeted promotions
Expand digital payment incentives to strengthen UPI adoption
📌 Conclusion

This analysis highlights key operational challenges within ride completion and cancellation management while also showcasing strong digital payment adoption and demand variability.

By addressing these issues, Uber can significantly improve service efficiency, customer satisfaction, and overall revenue performance.
